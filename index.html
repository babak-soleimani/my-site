MUSIC WRITING MAPS

I work across music, spatial research, and cultural analysis. My practice is concerned with how interiors shape listening — how light, surfaces, and arrangement can expand or compress the sense of presence between bodies.

I perform with AgawDilim, a project blending modal improvisation, microtonal drift, and rhythmic architectures influenced by jazz and regional classical traditions.

Alongside performance, I study the design histories of cafes and social rooms. Using open cultural datasets (including Yelp), I map the shift from intimate, shadowed “nook” interiors to the contemporary “airspace” aesthetic — the globalized coffee-shop interior typology of bright walls, hanging plants, and remote-worker neutrality.

My current work explores how aesthetic forms circulate through supply chains, how style encodes aspiration, and how listening can function as a method of reading space.
